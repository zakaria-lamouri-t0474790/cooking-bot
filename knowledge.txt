=== COOKING TIPS ===

1. KEEP YOUR KNIVES SHARP
   A sharp knife is safer and more effective than a dull one. It lets you cut ingredients more precisely and quickly.

2. USE FRESH HERBS
   Fresh herbs like mint, parsley, and coriander enhance flavor and add color to meals. Add them at the end of cooking for the best taste.

3. PREHEAT YOUR PAN
   Start with a hot pan before adding oil or ingredients. This prevents food from sticking and helps it cook evenly.

4. SEASON AS YOU GO
   Do not wait until the end to season. Add small amounts of salt, pepper, and spices during cooking so flavors build gradually.

5. LET MEAT REST
   After cooking meat, let it rest for 5-10 minutes. This allows juices to redistribute, making it tender and juicy.

6. CLEAN AS YOU COOK
   Keep your workspace clean by washing dishes and wiping counters as you go. It saves time and reduces stress later.

7. USE THE RIGHT OIL
   For high-heat cooking, use oils like sunflower or canola. For dressings or drizzling, use olive oil for better flavor.

8. TASTE FREQUENTLY
   Tasting while cooking helps you adjust seasoning and know when food is done. Do not be afraid to experiment.

9. READ THE RECIPE FIRST
   Always read the entire recipe before starting to cook. It helps avoid surprises and missing ingredients.

10. DO NOT CROWD THE PAN
    Give food space to cook. Crowding traps steam and causes uneven browning or soggy textures.
